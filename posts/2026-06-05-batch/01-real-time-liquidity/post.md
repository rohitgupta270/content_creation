# Post 1: Everyone Says Real-Time Payments Are About Speed. They're Wrong.

**Method:** Trend Response | **Pillar:** Future of Payments
**Format:** Text + Personal Photo | **Source:** Fintech Inside
**Status:** Ready to publish

There's a narrative problem in payments.

Everyone talks about real-time payments like they're about speed: "Instant settlement! No waiting!"

The companies actually winning with real-time payments aren't selling speed. They're selling working capital.

I realized this when a merchant asked me: "Why would I care if my payment settles in 2 hours vs. 2 days if I'm not using the money for 2 weeks anyway?"

He had a point. Speed of settlement only matters if you need the cash right now. For most merchants, that's not true.

What is true: If I can predict my cash flow more accurately, I can optimize it. I can pay my suppliers differently. I can manage my payroll better. I can take better advantage of supplier discounts because I know when money is hitting my account.

That's working capital optimization. That's liquidity management. That's the real value of real-time payments.

**Here's the proof:**

Wise (formerly TransferWise) shifted their B2B positioning from "instant international transfers" to "working capital optimization for SMBs." They added cash flow forecasting and payables integration. Result: B2B revenue grew 45% YoY, now 30% of total revenue, dominating in Europe and Southeast Asia.

Square saw the same pattern. They repositioned Square Cash for small business from "get paid faster" to "cash flow intelligence." Integrated with their Point of Sale system and accounting (Square Books). Their SMB segment (under $500k ARR) grew to 60% of merchant base. Average merchant lifetime value increased 3.2x.

Compare to companies still selling speed: Stripe Connect (focused on velocity), Square's competitor Clover (messaging instant payouts), and newer entrants Petal and Forward Bank. All strong products. None have achieved the market penetration of Wise or Square's position because they're competing on a commoditized feature.

**Here's what actually changed:**

They stopped selling a feature (speed). They started selling an outcome (cash flow optimization).

At a payments company I advised, we tested two positioning versions for their B2B product:

Version A: "Real-time settlement. Get your money faster." (competitor messaging)
Version B: "Forecast your cash position weekly. Pay suppliers on optimal timing. See liquidity 30 days out." (outcome messaging)

Same underlying payment rails. Different packaging.

Version B converted 3x better with mid-market (companies with $2-50M revenue). Why? Because $15M revenue companies have acute cash flow problems. Working capital is their constraint. They don't care about 2-hour vs. 2-day settlement. They care about knowing they'll have cash to make payroll.

**The data:**

According to McKinsey's 2024 payments survey, 67% of B2B companies cite "visibility into cash flow" as their top payments priority. Only 12% mention "settlement speed."

Yet 78% of payments startups lead with speed.

**The uncomfortable truth:**

If you're selling real-time payments as a feature, you're competing on speed. And speed is table stakes now. Square, Stripe, Wise—everyone's fast enough.

If you're selling real-time payments as a liquidity management tool, you're solving an actual business problem that 2 out of 3 companies desperately need.

The companies that own that narrative—Wise in cross-border B2B, Square in SMB payments—dominate their segments.

The ones still talking about speed are just selling infrastructure.
