# The Hardest Product Decisions Aren't About Features. They're About Identity.

Every roadmap is a bet on what your company will become.

Which means every roadmap is also a statement about what your company refuses to become.

I've watched teams treat roadmapping like feature prioritization. "Which 10 things maximize revenue?" Pick those. Ship them. Done.

Wrong lens.

The real question is: "If we ship these 10 things instead of the 10 we didn't pick, who are we as a company in 18 months?"

Stripe made an identity choice early: "We'll never charge per API call. Ever." That refused tens of millions in potential revenue. It's an identity choice that defined everything that came after. Developers knew they could trust Stripe's pricing model. Competitors who optimized for unit economics couldn't match that.

Airbnb chose: "We're not a property management company. We're an experience discovery platform." That meant saying no to features that would have made money but blurred the line.

These aren't product decisions. These are identity decisions. And they're executive conversations.

Most teams skip straight to prioritization without having the identity conversation. That's why roadmaps feel scattered. Everyone's building toward a different version of what the company should become.

Before your next prioritization meeting, ask: "What does this roadmap say about who we are?"

If you can't answer that clearly, you don't have a roadmap. You have a feature backlog.
