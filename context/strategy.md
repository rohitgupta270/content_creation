## Content Quality Standards

### ALWAYS:
- **Pattern Recognition** — "I've seen this across X companies/situations"
- **Uncomfortable Truth** — What most PMs get wrong about this
- **Context-Dependency** — When this works, when it fails
- **What NOT to Do** — Lessons from failures (more valuable than "here's what to do")
- **Systems Thinking** — How this connects to org/product dynamics
- **Specific Examples** — Real numbers, real situations, real tradeoffs from your experience
- **Tone of Peer** — Speaking to other experienced PMs, not teaching juniors

### NEVER:
- Generic "best practices" advice ("Here's a framework...")
- Tactics without strategy
- Simple answers to complex problems
- "PM 101" level thinking
- Frameworks without context on when they fail
- Advice without acknowledging the tradeoffs

## Publishing Strategy

- **Method 1: Viral Replication** (60%) — Find proven posts in product/payments/travel/AI spaces, steal the packaging, adapt the substance with Rohit's voice
  - **IMPORTANT:** Only replicate posts that solve real problems and provide value. Not just trend observations.

- **Method 2: Trend Surfing** (25%) — Catch what's trending right now, BUT only if it connects to a real problem people face
  - Trend must link to an actionable insight or solution
  - Not just "AI is changing X" — but "Here's how to use AI to solve X"

- **Method 3: Pain Points** (15%) — Deep dive into real, everyday problems your audience faces with actionable solutions
  - Problem: Humanly relatable (not abstract)
  - Solution: Concrete, actionable steps
  - Value: Readers can implement something tomorrow

## Value Delivery (Non-Negotiable)

Every post must follow this structure:

1. **Problem Statement** — Specific, relatable, real problem (not a trend observation)
2. **Context/Story** — Why this matters, specific example, or scenario
3. **Solution** — Actionable approach, framework, or concrete steps
4. **Takeaway** — What readers do differently tomorrow

**Posts that FAIL this structure:**
- ❌ Identify a problem but don't solve it
- ❌ Comment on trends without actionable value
- ❌ Abstract observations with no practical use
- ❌ Leave readers thinking "so what?" instead of "I can do this tomorrow"
