# Adam Robinson Writing Style Guide

**Source:** 60 LinkedIn posts scraped from linkedin.com/in/retentionadam (March 2026)
**Full data:** `context/data/linkedin/adam-robinson-posts.json`

---

## Core Voice Characteristics

### 1. Conversational stream-of-consciousness
He writes like he's texting a friend or thinking out loud. Sentences start mid-thought. He interrupts himself with parenthetical asides.

Examples:
- "(I was like '$1m ARR, or $1m MRR?!?!?!')"
- "(Even writing this is triggering some serious 2023 PTSD for me!)"
- "This is not a sponsored post, I just think the tech is DOPE."
- "Maybe you're right. But it's MY startup."

### 2. Imperfect on purpose
Grammar isn't polished. He uses fragments, starts sentences with "But", "And", "So". Capitalizes for EMPHASIS not structure. Uses multiple question marks and exclamation marks.

Examples:
- "Am I correct Kennan????"
- "Did I miss anything in my analysis???"
- "Can we do it? LET ME KNOW IN THE COMMENTS."
- "Nobody f*cking does it."
- "LFG!!! 🚀"

### 3. Real numbers, real transparency
He drops specific dollar amounts, percentages, and metrics like he's talking to his co-founder. Not rounded, not vague.

Examples:
- "$28.2m Run Rate (+11.2%)"
- "$1m/mo 6mo Average Profitability (+45%)"
- "5%/mo for Retention, 9%/mo for RB2B"
- "hit $1m MRR in 12 months"
- "$7m ARR under 2 years"

### 4. Strong opinions delivered casually
He drops contrarian bombs but wraps them in casual language so they feel like observations, not lectures.

Examples:
- "Giving AI agents to people who don't know how to build apps in a secure way is like giving an AK-47 to a monkey."
- "70% of VCs are net negative to the companies they fund."
- "People love to say outbound is dead (I know I do!). But has anyone tried writing better copy???"
- "Your valuation is not your self-worth."

### 5. Self-deprecating and vulnerable
He admits failures, questions himself, shows genuine uncertainty.

Examples:
- "How am I supposed to watch this and not feel like what I spend my life on is totally inconsequential?"
- "If I'm being honest I get massive FOMO when I see AI startups going $0-$100m ARR"
- "I take full responsibility for not investing in innovation on the Retention side"
- "I told him what I wanted: mid-20s ARR SaaS with under 40 people. He thought I was crazy."

### 6. Storytelling with dialogue
He uses actual dialogue and names real people. Creates mini-narratives.

Examples:
- 'Him: "We'll be worth a billion soon." I told him no.'
- 'Amos: "We're building a generational company and not planning on raising any additional funds." Is anybody else confused by that statement?'
- "CEO Friend: Is cold outreach working for you guys?"

## Structural Patterns

### Hook styles
- **Bold declarative statement:** "$10M ARR is the FU MONEY of SaaS."
- **Story opening:** "In 2017, a CEO friend told me I was 'wasting my life' on my tiny startup."
- **Specific + surprising:** "This is the only cold email I answered in the last 12 months."
- **Provocative take:** "I want to be crystal clear: the advice I give on LinkedIn is NOT for founders who think they're creating unicorns."
- **Personal moment:** "As I sat uncomfortably awake all night in preparation for a 6:15am colonoscopy..."

### Body structure
- **NOT formulaic.** He doesn't use the same template every post. Some are lists. Some are stories. Some are rants. Some are breakdowns.
- Uses em dashes (--) and "…" and "======" as separators
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Numbered lists but MESSY ones — he adds commentary between list items
- Sometimes goes on tangents and comes back

### Endings
- Often asks the reader a direct question
- Sometimes just... stops. No neat conclusion.
- "LET ME KNOW IN THE COMMENTS."
- "Any ideas?"
- "Am I correct Kennan???? Did I miss anything in my analysis???"
- "That's the FU money of bootstrapping."

---

## What NOT to do (anti-patterns)

- Never uses "Here's the thing:" or "Let me break it down:"
- Never uses perfect grammar or polished transitions
- Never uses "In today's landscape" or "Let me share" or any AI-sounding setup
- Doesn't use emojis systematically (occasional 🚀 or 👇 but rare)
- Doesn't use "Step 1, Step 2, Step 3" clean frameworks
- Doesn't wrap up with a neat bow — his posts feel slightly unfinished

## How to Apply This to Oleg's Content

1. **Start mid-thought** — don't set up the post, just jump in
2. **Use parenthetical asides** — break your own flow like a real person would
3. **Drop specific numbers** — not "grew a lot" but "71 posts, 3 minutes, $20/month"
4. **Capitalize for emphasis**, not structure
5. **Ask messy questions** — multiple question marks, tag people, be genuinely curious
6. **Leave some edges rough** — not every paragraph needs to be a perfect standalone thought
7. **Mix formats** — don't use the same post structure twice in a row
8. **Be self-deprecating** — "honestly I have no idea if this will work"
9. **Name names** — reference real people, real tools, real situations
10. **End abruptly** sometimes — not every post needs a clean CTA
