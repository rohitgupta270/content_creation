# Travel Tech's Biggest Mistake: Solving the Wrong Problem at Scale

The travel industry's obsession is misplaced.

Everyone's racing to solve "give users more options" faster. More flights, more filters, more customization. Skyscanner showed it: 10,000 flight options with endless filtering led to a 4% conversion decline year-over-year.

Meanwhile, Hopper took a different approach: "What if we just picked the best option for you?" One recommendation, based on your preferences. Conversion lift: 31%.

Here's what I've seen happen repeatedly: When founders come from tech, they solve for complexity. When they come from travel, they solve for overwhelm.

The traveler's actual problem isn't more information. It's decision fatigue. After 20 minutes of scrolling, most people abandon. Not because they couldn't find what they wanted. Because they couldn't decide.

The companies winning in travel right now aren't building better search. They're building better *curation*. Booking.com's "For you" recommendations. Airbnb's curated collections. Tripadvisor's "Best time to visit" narratives.

This isn't just UX. It's a product philosophy: Trust the algorithm more than the user's ability to sift through noise.

The highest conversion doesn't come from maximum choice. It comes from confident guidance.
