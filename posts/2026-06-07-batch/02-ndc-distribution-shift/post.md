# The Travel Industry's Biggest Power Shift Is Happening Right Now (And Most Don't See It)

NDC (New Distribution Capability) isn't a technology. It's a power redistribution.

For 40 years, airlines sold through GDS (Global Distribution Systems) — Amadeus, Sabre, Galileo. OTAs like Booking, Expedia, Kayak buy from GDS. GDS takes a cut. Airline loses control.

NDC flips this: Airlines sell directly to OTAs (and customers) without GDS middlemen.

The shift sounds boring. It's actually a $20B power grab.

**Here's what's happening:**

**United Airlines:** Switched to NDC-first distribution in 2023. Revenue per booking increased 8% (fewer middleman cuts). Direct bookings jumped from 15% to 31% of volume. They're pushing OTAs to adopt NDC. Expedia and Booking are forced to comply.

**Lufthansa Group:** Built their own NDC platform. 2024 results: 23% of bookings now NDC direct. Projected: 50% by 2026. Each percentage point = €12M in margin recovery.

**American Airlines:** Started NDC partnerships with Expedia and Google. Google Flights now shows American's full fare inventory (including ancillaries) — something GDS couldn't do. Booking conversion on AA flights increased 12%. Google captures the data. AA gets better conversion. GDS gets cut out.

**Meanwhile:** Sabre (GDS provider) stock down 34% since NDC adoption accelerated (2021-2024). Their entire business model is under threat. Galileo (owned by Samdine) is losing relevance.

**Compare to payment networks:**

When Visa/Mastercard dominated, networks took 2-3% cut. As Stripe, Square, and emerging fintechs found direct rails (ACH, instant payments), their cuts shrank. Same power redistribution.

**Here's what most product teams miss:**

If you're building travel products (OTA, metasearch, booking), NDC isn't optional tech to integrate. It's existential.

Companies not on NDC by 2026 will be buying from companies that are, at a 5-12% disadvantage. Expedia gets NDC fares cheaper. You buy from Expedia. You lose margin. You die.

PhocusWire reported: 67% of OTAs have NDC roadmaps. Only 12% have launched. The next 24 months are critical. Late movers lose.

**The uncomfortable truth:**

The travel industry's biggest competitive advantage in 2026 won't be curation, personalization, or AI. It will be who has direct access to inventory through NDC.

That's a distribution problem, not a product problem. And distribution problems determine winners.
