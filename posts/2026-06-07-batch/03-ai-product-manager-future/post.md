# AI Won't Replace Product Managers. It Will Replace the Wrong Kind of PM Work.

Here's what I actually think is happening:

Product managers spend their day doing things that AI does better:
- Writing 30-page strategy documents (Claude drafts them in 5 minutes)
- Summarizing meetings and Slack threads (happens automatically now)
- Creating roadmaps and updating Jira (AI can handle status updates, prioritization logic)
- Writing copy for feature announcements (AI writes better copy than most teams)

So yes, AI will replace those. But that's not PM work. That's admin work that somehow became "PM" because we've scaled processes instead of scaling judgment.

The PM work that matters:
- Talking to users and asking the right follow-up questions
- Making trade-off decisions in ambiguity
- Reading a room and building consensus
- Saying no to good ideas for strategic reasons

These things require judgment. Taste. Conviction. Things AI can help with, but not replace.

McKinsey found this: Companies using AI for discovery (qualitative analysis at scale) + human judgment saw 2.3x faster shipping than teams trying to let AI decide.

The future of product management isn't PMs-as-AI-prompters. It's PMs who use AI to do the commodity work faster, so they can spend time on the judgment work that actually matters.

Your value isn't disappearing. It's shifting. Better understand which side you want to be on.
