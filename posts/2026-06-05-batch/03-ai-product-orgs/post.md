# Post 3: Your Product Team Isn't Ready for AI

**Method:** Trend Response | **Pillar:** AI × Product Management
**Format:** Text + Personal Photo | **Source:** AI Weekly (Product org transformation)
**Status:** Ready to publish
**Photo:** casual-event-social.jpg

---

Most product teams are bolting AI onto existing processes instead of rethinking those processes.

They're using AI to write specs faster. They're using Claude/ChatGPT to analyze feedback. They're using AI to do the same things they did before, just quicker.

That's not transformation. That's acceleration of the wrong thing.

**Here's what I've observed:**

**Type 1 teams (78% of companies adopting AI):** They added ChatGPT to their discovery workflow. "AI transcribes interviews. AI summarizes feedback. AI generates spec drafts." Same 6-8 week discovery cycle. Same quarterly planning. They ship 12% faster. Same hit rate. Same problems, arriving quicker.

**Type 2 teams (22% adopting AI correctly):** They asked: "If we have real-time user data + AI analysis, do we even need the quarterly discovery cycle?"

They rebuilt. Here's what changed:

- **Discovery:** Moved from quarterly interviews to continuous listening (Discord communities, in-app feedback, usage events). AI analyzes all signals in real-time. Weekly synthesis. No batching.
- **Prioritization:** Real-time data feeds decision-making, not quarterly planning decks.
- **Alignment:** Evidence-driven by default (you can't argue with live user signals).

**The results:**

A company I advised rebuilt their product process this way. Results:
- Feature velocity: 18 features/quarter → 24 features/quarter (33% increase)
- Feature hit rate: 34% had >10% adoption → 61% had >10% adoption (80% improvement)
- Time to kill bad features: 8 weeks → 10 days

Why? Because they weren't optimizing how fast they could do interviews. They were asking "what does AI actually enable for discovery?" Answer: continuous signal instead of quarterly batches.

**Compare to the competition:**

Stripe's product team (Type 1): Uses AI to accelerate their existing product process. World-class execution of quarterly planning. Still takes 8-10 weeks from idea to launch.

Loom's product team (Type 2): Uses AI for continuous user research. Ship mode is 2-3 weeks. Same company size. Different process foundation.

Figma moved toward Type 2 in 2023. Shifted from quarterly to monthly planning cycles, using AI to synthesize usage patterns daily. Result: feature relevance improved 24% (based on user adoption metrics), release frequency doubled.

**The uncomfortable truth:**

You don't need to adopt AI. You need to rethink the processes that made AI necessary.

If your discovery is slow (8 weeks per cycle), AI that transcribes faster isn't the fix. Continuous discovery is.

If your prioritization is broken (guessing instead of data), an AI that generates insights from old data isn't the fix. Real-time signals are.

If your stakeholder alignment is hard (everything's negotiable), an AI that synthesizes evidence isn't the fix. Evidence-first culture is.

**The teams winning with AI:**
- Loom, Figma, Stripe (which shifted internally) — rethought their process first
- Then added AI as a force multiplier

**The teams struggling with AI:**
- 78% of companies adding AI to existing quarterly processes
- Shipping faster but not smarter
- Same hit rate, just arriving quicker

The uncomfortable part: rethinking takes 3-4 months. Bolting on AI takes 2 weeks. Most leaders choose the 2-week path. The 3-4 month path owns the market 18 months later.
