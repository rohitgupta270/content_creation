# Real-Time Payments Won't Succeed Until Companies Solve the Trust Problem

Most teams building payment products are solving the wrong problem.

They're focused on speed. "We can settle in 2 seconds instead of 2 days." That's table stakes now, not differentiation.

The real problem: People don't trust instant settlements because they can't see what happened.

I've watched this pattern repeat across three companies I've worked with. Merchants would shift to real-time payment rails, but adoption stalled. Why? Because when a $50,000 wire hits your account instantly, and something goes wrong, you have no trail. Traditional banking gave you documentation, clearing houses, dispute mechanisms. Real-time payments took those away.

Wise figured this out. They didn't just build speed. They built confidence layers:
- Transparency into what's actually happening (micro-FX rates shown in real-time)
- Clear dispute resolution (who covers losses if something breaks)
- API documentation that answers "what if this fails" before it fails

The companies winning in real-time payments aren't the fastest. They're the ones who rebuilt trust for a world where money moves instantly.

This is what separates the $11B valuations from the also-rans. Not velocity. Architecture for trust at velocity.
