# I've Moved Between Four Countries in 15 Years. Here's What It Taught Me About Product Decisions.

The biggest learning wasn't about moving. It was about assumptions.

I started my career in India assuming Indian users want cheap. Then I moved to Europe and realized they want reliability. Then to Spain where I learned they want simplicity. None of these assumptions held across borders.

And the scariest part: Most products optimize for one regional assumption and call it "global."

I've watched teams build payment products optimized for "users want instant settlement" (very North American). Then they ship to India where users want transparency over speed. Or Brazil where users want offline resilience because connectivity isn't guaranteed.

The lesson wasn't about geography. It was about operating without testing your assumptions.

Product decisions that work in one market feel broken in another. Not because of geography. Because of decision-making rigor.

When I moved countries three times, I had to learn the same lesson three times: "We know how users behave" is the most dangerous assumption in product.

The teams I've seen ship globally well don't build one product and export it. They build the core conviction, then run lean discovery in each market to test where their assumption breaks.

After 15 years, I'm still learning this: Scale doesn't come from better prediction. It comes from faster assumption testing.

Where's your team testing its biggest assumption?
